Title: Thinking about living in Identity Logan Park? Read this.
Source: r/berkeley thread, https://www.reddit.com/r/berkeley/comments/1abwegr/thinking_about_living_in_identity_logan_park_read/
Posted: 2024-01-26

Original post:
I've seen a lot of people touring Identity Logan Park recently, and I figured I'd give my two cents about Identity as someone who's lived here for about two years now.

**Cost:** Identity's pricing is very high. Their most common floorplan is 2 bedroom + 2 bedroom. These apartments start at around $1400 per person, which doesn't sound terrible, especially with the way that they advertise. Yeah, you're paying $1400 a month to share a room with someone, but on the bright side, you get lots of amenities and a fully furnished apartment... I'll get to the amenities and furnishing in a later section. There are very few 3 bed + 3 bath apartments available, so I won't touch on that, but the 1 bed and studio pricings are abysmal. Studios start at around $3500 and are intended to be for one person. 1 Bedroom apartments, intended to be a double, start at around $1600. One thing to note is when I say start, most of the starter apartments have already been rented, and they charge more for rooms that are on higher floors (I'm not kidding), so expect to be paying in the range of $1800 - $2000 a month for a shared 1 bedroom apartment. This doesn't sound like the complete worst, until you get to power bills.

**Furnishings (in bedroom):** The furnishings in the apartments, much like Identity's entire brand image, looks great on the outside, but is cheap and falls apart once you actually use it for a bit. Bedrooms are furnished with the following:

* Desks
* Chairs
* Beds
* Wardrobes
* Dresser

The desks are the least egregious part as the only drawback on these is that they are small, and some of my bigger friends (taller than 5' 6") find them to be very uncomfortable. The chairs are incredibly cheaply built and in my time living here, 3 of the 4 chairs the apartment came with have broken from regular use. Another thing to note, is Identity will find a way to charge you for everything. Each of these chairs caused my roommates to incur a charge of $60 for a replacement. For reference, all 4 of our chairs were wobbly and somewhat falling apart when we moved in. The Beds are hit or miss. Some of the beds here are great, but others have had the bed frames break or become very loud and wobbly. These cost $100+ to repair. The wardrobes in the rooms are not properly measured or installed, so many of the doors don't even open all the way, and some will block the door to your room from opening. The dressers are by far the worst. I moved in with two of the drawers snapped off, and they wanted to charge me to have the dresser doors fixed. I still have a broken dresser.

**Furnishings (in apartment):** The apartments come with the following:

* Tall chairs
* Couch
* Television
* Washer + Drier Unit
* Dishwasher
* Stove + Oven
* Microwave
* Refrigerator

The tall chairs suffer from the same issue as the desk chairs, most of mine have broken. The couches are hit or miss. When I moved in, my couch was covered in hair and dirt, and I had to ask maintenance to wash it. They said it would cost money. I cleaned the couch myself. The washer and drier are actually pretty good, good job Identity! My dishwasher has never worked in my entire time living here, it turns on, but doesn't clean dishes very well at all. I've tried many different detergents to no avail. The stove, oven and refrigerator are all fully functional, but they drain an insane amount of power, which brings me to the previously alluded to point.

**Cost of power:** On average in a 2 bed + 2 bath apartment, my power bill is about $200 a month. Split 4 ways, this isn't terrible, but it hurts knowing that my friends in Varsity and the Standard pay about $30 a month. You may be asking, what do you have that is using so much power? It's the kitchen appliances. In July, while all of my roommates and I were not living there, our power bill was $150. My mistake for not unplugging my oven before moving out.

**Amenities:** Identity boasts the following amenities in their advertisements:

* "24-Hour Fitness Center"
* "Private Flex Fitness Room with Yoga and Fitness On-Demand"
* "Rooftop Sky Lounge"
* "Trellis Lounge with TV, Fire Pits, and Grills"
* "Sky Lounge with Kitchenette"
* "Game Zone"
* "Courtyards"
* "Dog Park"
* "Coffee &amp; Breakfast Bar"

These sound pretty good, but let's take a look at what each entails. The 24-Hour Fitness Center is a medium sized gym that has a number of treadmills, ellipticals, dumbbells, and six different weight training machines. Each of the weight training machines has a different issue with how it's being maintained, DM me for specifics if you care, and there was a 3 month stretch last semester (Fall '23) where four of the six machines were completely broken and out of commission. The rooftop sky lounge has a great view, which is nice, but more often than not, the furniture on the roof will often be soiled or broken from parties. Identity has recently announced that parties are no longer allowed, but the furniture has not been fixed yet. This goes for the Courtyards as well. All of the lounges are often commandeered by consulting clubs for meetings, so I wouldn't plan on being able to use these if you live here. The so called game zone, is a small room with a miniature pool table that can convert to be a ping pong table. The ping pong attachment is broken. The dog park is a tiny patch of grass on the roof and in my two years here, I've seen one dog there. The coffee and breakfast bar has got to be the biggest bait of all of these though. The coffee machine is often broken, and the 'breakfast' is something they offer once or twice a semester.

**Parking:** Identity advertises that they offer affordable and readily available parking spaces in a secure garage. Every aspect of the sentence I just stated is a lie. First of all, **parking costs $300 a month.** They also won't tell you it costs this much until you already have a car there with you, in an attempt to strong arm you into spending too much money on parking. For reference, parking at the apartment down the street at the corner of Milvia and Durant costs $60 a month. The cars are stored in a [Klaus](https://klausparking.com/category/semi-automatic-system/) parking system. It's a pretty cool piece of technology, until you actually use it. It takes anywhere from 3-10 minutes to get your car out of the garage, of which the gate is currently broken, and the Klaus parking system breaks 3-4 times a month. It takes on average an entire business day to fix the parking system which creates two awful scenarios. If your car is in the machine while it's broken, good luck. You won't be able to get your car out until it get's repaired. This sound bad, but if your car isn't in the machine when it breaks is somehow worse. First of all, you aren't allowed to park in the regular spots in the garage as they're reserved for staff and employees at the connected Chase bank. If you do park in these spots, they have all non-registered cars towed at the end of the day and issue a fine of $500 on top of having to get your car from a tow lot. Instead, you have to pay out of pocket for street parking until it gets fixed. This has happened to me twice, and Identity has declined responsibility for reimbursing me both times.

**General Things:**

* The trash rooms are often overflowing with trash and the scent will get into the hallway. They also don't allow for recycling in the trash rooms, because it costs too much money. This is a real thing the building manager told me when I asked around the time I moved in.
* The mailroom will often lose packages, and depending on who's working, they may tell you that your package hasn't been delivered when in reality, they are just too lazy to look for it.
* The maintenance licenses in the elevators expired in **December of 2022**.
* The fire alarm will false alarm 3-8 times a month on average.
* Once you move in, they will very aggressively try to get you to extend your lease. They will call you every single day and then try to guild trip you into renewing, even if you ask them to stop contacting you.
* Maintenance for broken appliances is very spotty. My friend had his fridge break and had to wait over a month to get it fixed.
* The staff is trained to deflect blame and guilt trip tenants.

**Summary:** Just about everything at Identity is false advertising. From the 'gym' that doesn't work to the 'premium furnishings' that break after 5 uses, it's all a clever ploy to steal money from the tenants. If you are a student who is looking for housing next year and are anxious about not finding a place to live, it's alright. Although housing is incredibly hard to find in Berkeley, living in a place like Identity is not the answer. It would be one thing if it was really expensive, another if it smelled like literal feces in the hallways, or arguably okay if just the staff was obnoxious. Instead, it's all of the above. I will restate this in the least ambiguous way possible.

**tl;dr DO NOT LIVE HERE.**
