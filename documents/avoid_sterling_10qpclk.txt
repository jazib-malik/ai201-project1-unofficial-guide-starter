Since apartment hunting season is coming up. I thought I'll expose these \*\*\*\*\*\*\*\* once and for all.

Here's a google review I wrote 1 month ago:

1. There are mandatory trash collection which you have to pay for and are billed for monthly. They only collect in the late afternoon for around 2 hours, and if they catch you having the trash outside the "collection period" you will be fined. They don't even collect the trash half the time.. 2. The apartments themselves are horribly designed. Storage areas are either inconvenient or are way too small. They even have "fake" storage areas that look it'll be useful but inside 90% of the room is taken up by something. 3. Lying and bad management. Asked if there would be fans in the apartment, they said yes, but guess what. There's no fans. Was promised a replacement for something within 2 weeks, but after 3 months, still nothing. I've had numerous packages stolen when delivery left it in the mailroom which had security cameras. I contacted the management about this issue and requested security footage to see who is stealing my packages, but they refused and ignored my request. (note: the security camera is ON and is pointing directly at where my package was left, I know this because I had to go to class and saw my package on the way out, but when I came back it was missing. What's the point of having these cameras if they aren't going to use it? Now whenever I order something expensive, I have to go downstairs to pick it up right away or else it gets stolen. I think the fastest time a package was stolen from me was 30 minutes after I received the delivered confirmation.  4. Security issues. The apartment garage door was broken for several weeks, but instead of repairing it, they simply left it open. Over these weeks, bicycles were stolen with the locks cut. Numerous residents contacted management about this but were all ignored.  5. random inconveniences: the apartment is "gated" and you need to use a key fob to get in. That means, no food delivery because they don't have key fobs so you would need to go downstairs to pick it up. That isn't too big of an issue, but what is, is that any delivery service that isn't amazon, fedex, ups or usps, cannot get into the apartment. So unless you want your package to sit outside in downtown berkeley (not recommended) you would need to be there whenever the delivery person arrives or else too bad. Wifi is included but that also means your not allowed to get different plans or different service providers. Internet here is more unreliable than campus wifi, it constantly freezes/disconnects.

Outside of building always smells like weed and there's a big construction next to us.

Elevator feels like it's about to break anytime now, I straight up don't trust it anymore and just take the stairs.

Roof top just started renovation couple weeks ago even though management said they would start like 5 months ago and be finished by now.
