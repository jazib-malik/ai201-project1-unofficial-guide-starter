Title: Best neighborhoods for CS major (off campus housing)?

Posted: 2021-05-17

Original post:
Hi, I am a rising CS sophomore and am taking classes like 170, 16B, 126 next sem for fall 2021. I was wondering if anyone has any advice for which neighborhoods are the safest and closest to CS classes in general for off campus housing? Also any advice on how many additional people I should room with (single is not an option for me)? 

I have checked Cal rentals and have created a profile.

Replies from students:

[9 points] Northside, specifically Soda would host a few more CS classes.  16B labs though will be online for Fall and discussion sections could be closer to the center as well, so the point is that you can make it work regardless of where you are at.  Northside will generally be safer than Southside, but don’t take any place for granted.

[3 points] I don’t think there is any set number.  I did set a parameter for my kid last year, primarily due to Covid, that he find a single.  So he wound up in a 4 bedroom place with 3 others, which worked out ok.  I would start from there (finding singles) and see what the costs are.  You should be able to pay max at around $1500-1600/month per person on Northside, lower in Southside.

[2 points] Thank you for your advice! Do you have any guidance for how many people is a good number to room with?

[2 points] Northside is the closest but it really doesn't matter that much, your classes will probably be spread out enough to make it irrelevant. Just choose the area you like the most (northside is slightly safer but quieter, more boring, more expensive, southside has the most stuff to do and the most students and is the cheapest, downtown somewhere in between). You can room with however many people you want, depends on your budget and how many people you're comfortable with staying with you. The more people that stay in your room, the cheaper it will be for you, but you'll also get less privacy. Doubles and triples are the most common though fwiw.
