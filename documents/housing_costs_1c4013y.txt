Original post:
TLDR: more housing good, more expensive housing bad. 

I’m a rising senior now and I’m in a very fortunate situation in which I was able to find affordable and secure housing immediately after freshman year.

I’m extremely glad and excited that all the housing projects that I’ve been seeing around campus are finally starting to finish, such as Kittredge and the Anchor House, since we are in desperate need for more housing.

However, I am slight wary/pessimistic for the future of housing in Berkeley.

It feels like every time some new apartment near campus is built, they always stick the “luxury” tag on it to charge exorbitant prices. I don’t remember the name of the places (probably not too hard to find though) but I remember when I was looking for housing freshman year, there were luxury apartment complexes on Southside that were charging 1,000-1,500$ per bed in triples and quads (rooms). For example I commonly saw 1bd/1ba for like 4,000<, and they got away with it by saying 1,400 “per bed” because they plan for the 1 room in the apartment to be a triple.

It just feels really scummy. Like they know the prices are awful, but they can just say “it’s affordable if you have roommates.” Like if you look at the bedroom designs for those luxury apartments, they will have bunk beds and/or two to three beds in frame.

And the Anchor House looks extremely nice, but I fear that because it’s new and shiny (“luxury”), there will be an even heftier up charge to live there then there was to live in Blackwell (compared to the other units, which were already expensive).

It feels very dirty that we are in such a housing crisis, yet instead of providing more affordable housing options both the university and surrounding apartment companies are trying to profit from the situation.

I feel conflicted though, since we desperately need housing. And the price to live at these luxury apartments (if you don’t mind doubles and triples) is still cheap compared to university housing prices. 

I’d like to hear different opinions about the market though, since I’m currently in my own thought box.


Edit - just looked at the prices for Anchor, and it’s not that bad compared to other university housing and considering whats offered. It’s still much cheaper to find a non-luxury, non-university apartment off campus though.
